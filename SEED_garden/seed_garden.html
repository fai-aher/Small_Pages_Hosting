About the SEED Garden

The SEED Garden is a new and innovative approach to the SEED Alumni Project for 2025. It reimagines our community as a thriving ecosystem where each participant contributes to a beautiful, diverse, and productive network of relationships that span across countries, cohorts, and cultures.

Through this initiative, we aim to foster meaningful connections between current SEED 2025 participants and alumni from previous years (2022-2024), creating a supportive community that continues to grow long after the program ends. The SEED Garden combines professional development, cultural exchange, and mutual support in a gamified structure that rewards engagement and contribution.

Key objectives of the SEED Garden include:

  • Strengthening connections between alumni across all cohorts
  • Providing mentorship opportunities for current participants
  • Supporting Yamanashi producers through continued engagement
  • Creating valuable networking opportunities across countries
  • Maintaining and deepening everyone's connection to Japanese culture
  • Building Professional Groves - specialized communities where alumni with similar academic backgrounds, career goals, or professional interests can collaborate and share insights

Join us in cultivating this unique community where everyone can grow, share knowledge, and build lasting professional relationships!

Growth Stages

The SEED Garden employs a gamified engagement system where each participant begins as a seed and progresses through various growth stages by earning points. This structure provides clear goals and recognition for your contributions to the community.

At the Young Tree stage (400 points), you'll discover which type of tree you'll become, adding an element of excitement and personalization to your journey. As you continue to participate and contribute, your tree will blossom and reach mythical status!

Professional Groves

Professional Groves are specialized communities within the SEED Garden where alumni with similar academic backgrounds, career paths, or professional interests can connect in more intimate spaces. These groves facilitate deeper conversations, knowledge sharing, and collaborative opportunities among like-minded individuals.

Current Professional Groves include:

  • Engineering Grove: For engineers and technical professionals
  • Business Grove: For entrepreneurs, consultants, and business professionals
  • Design Grove: For designers, artists, and creative professionals
  • Academic Grove: For researchers, professors, and those in academic careers
  • Social Impact Grove: For those working in NGOs, social enterprises, and sustainability
  • Technology Grove: For software developers, data scientists, and tech innovators

Participation in Professional Groves provides additional networking opportunities and specialized mentorship that complements the broader SEED Garden activities.

How to Participate

Participating in the SEED Garden is easy and rewarding! Here's how to get started and make the most of this unique community:

  1. Register on the SEED Intranet: Ensure your profile is complete and up-to-date
  2. Attend the Welcome Ceremony: Join us on July 10th for the official launch
  3. Participate in Activities: Check the calendar regularly and join events that match your schedule
  4. Track Your Progress: Monitor your growth stage and points on the SEED Intranet real-time leaderboard
  5. Join Professional Groves: Connect with alumni in your field of expertise
  6. Give Feedback: Help us improve by sharing your thoughts on activities and the overall program

Remember that your participation not only helps you earn rewards but also creates tremendous value for current SEED participants who benefit from your experience and insights. Together, we can create a vibrant, supportive community that extends far beyond the formal SEED Program timeline.

Core Leadership Responsibilities

All SEED Garden leaders share fundamental responsibilities while bringing their unique expertise to the community.

Community Management

  • Active Communication: Maintain ongoing contact with all alumni from representing region
  • Cultural Education: Prepare and deliver 2-3 lectures on Japanese cultural topics
  • Network Building: Facilitate connections between alumni with similar interests
  • Feedback Collection: Gather input from participants to improve the program

Project Support

  • Activity Moderation: Serve as moderators for virtual and in-person activities
  • Project Development: Contribute ideas and assistance to improve program structure
  • Quality Assurance: Maintain standards for cultural education sessions
  • Mentorship: Provide guidance and support to current SEED 2025 participants

Decision-Making and Governance

The SEED Garden leadership operates on principles of collaborative decision-making, transparency, and mutual respect.

Collaborative Process

  1. Proposal Phase: Any leader can propose new initiatives through leadership channels
  2. Discussion Phase: Open discussion with feedback and suggestions from all leaders
  3. Refinement Phase: Proposals modified based on collective input
  4. Consensus Building: Work together to reach agreement ensuring all voices heard
  5. Implementation: Project Coordinator coordinates implementation with regional leaders

Authority Structure

  • Project Coordinator: Overall responsibility and primary liaison with GOROM Association
  • Regional Leaders: Autonomy in specific areas while adhering to program standards
  • Global Moderator: Cross-regional coordination and community standards enforcement
  • Emergency Decisions: Project Coordinator can make urgent decisions with two other leaders

Commitment Standards

  • Time Dedication: 3-4 hours weekly for community engagement and planning
  • Monthly Activities: Organize and lead 1-2 regional activities per month
  • Cultural Lectures: 5-6 hours preparation per lecture (2-3 annually)
  • Administrative Tasks: 1-2 hours weekly for reporting and coordination

Event Protocol & Guidelines

Standards and procedures for SEED Garden events and meetings

Virtual Events Protocol

Technical Requirements

  • Platform: Zoom (primary), Google Meet (backup)
  • Camera Policy: Cameras ON throughout the session (bonus points awarded)
  • Microphone: Mute when not speaking, unmute for participation
  • Internet: Stable connection required, test beforehand
  • Backup Plan: Phone dial-in available for technical issues

Participation Guidelines

  • Punctuality: Join 5 minutes before start time
  • Professional Setup: Good lighting, quiet environment, appropriate background
  • Engagement: Active participation in discussions and activities
  • Respect: Listen actively, wait for speaking turns, be culturally sensitive
  • Documentation: Take notes, questions welcome during designated times

In-Person Events Protocol

Event Organization

  • Venue Selection: Accessible, culturally appropriate, safe location
  • Capacity: Confirmed headcount 48 hours in advance
  • Materials: Name tags, printed materials, cultural items if applicable
  • Documentation: Photography/video with participant consent
  • Safety: Emergency contacts and procedures clearly communicated

Networking Standards

  • Introductions: Structured ice-breaker activities
  • Contact Exchange: Business cards, LinkedIn connections encouraged
  • Cultural Sensitivity: Respect for diverse backgrounds and customs
  • Follow-up: Leaders facilitate post-event connections
  • Inclusivity: Ensure all participants feel welcome and included

Cultural Workshop Standards

Cultural workshops are core components of the SEED Garden experience. All cultural activities must meet these standards:

  • Authenticity: Information must be accurate and culturally respectful
  • Engagement: Interactive elements to encourage participation
  • Duration: 45-60 minutes for virtual, 90-120 minutes for in-person
  • Materials: Provide resources for continued learning
  • Q&A Session: Dedicated time for questions and discussion
  • Cultural Context: Explain historical and social background
  • Practical Application: How knowledge applies to current SEED projects

Point Distribution Protocol

Attendance Verification

  • Check-in required at event start
  • Mid-event participation checkpoint
  • Completion confirmation for full points
  • Partial attendance = proportional points

Bonus Point Criteria

  • Camera on for 80%+ of virtual event (+20-30 pts)
  • Active participation in discussions (+10 pts)
  • Helping with event organization (+15 pts)
  • Bringing new participants (+25 pts)

Quality Standards

  • Professional behavior throughout
  • Constructive contribution to discussions
  • Respect for facilitators and participants
  • Adherence to scheduled timing

Data Privacy & Usage Policy

By registering for the SEED Garden 2025 program, you acknowledge and agree to the following:

  • You grant GOROM Association permission to collect, store, and use your personal information (name, email, university, country, professional background) for non-profit educational purposes related to the SEED Program
  • Your information will be used exclusively for SEED program activities, alumni network management, and promotional materials for future SEED editions
  • GOROM Association may use your photos, testimonials, and participation highlights in social media content, website materials, and program promotion with proper attribution
  • Your data will not be shared with third parties outside of GOROM Association and participating universities without your explicit consent
  • You have the right to request access to, modification of, or deletion of your personal data at any time by contacting the SEED Garden coordinators
  • Participation in the SEED Garden is voluntary, and you may withdraw from the program at any time

We are committed to protecting your privacy while building a vibrant, connected global community of SEED alumni.
