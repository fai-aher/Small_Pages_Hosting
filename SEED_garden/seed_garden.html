About the SEED Garden

The SEED Garden is a new and innovative approach to the SEED Alumni Project for 2025. It reimagines our community as a thriving ecosystem where each participant contributes to a beautiful, diverse, and productive network of relationships that span across countries, cohorts, and cultures.

Through this initiative, we aim to foster meaningful connections between current SEED 2025 participants and alumni from previous years (2022-2024), creating a supportive community that continues to grow long after the program ends. The SEED Garden combines professional development, cultural exchange, and mutual support in a gamified structure that rewards engagement and contribution.

Key objectives of the SEED Garden include:

  • Strengthening connections between alumni across all cohorts
  • Providing mentorship opportunities for current participants
  • Supporting Yamanashi producers through continued engagement
  • Creating valuable networking opportunities across countries
  • Maintaining and deepening everyone's connection to Japanese culture

Join us in cultivating this unique community where everyone can grow, share knowledge, and build lasting professional relationships!

Growth Stages

The SEED Garden employs a gamified engagement system where each participant begins as a seed and progresses through various growth stages by earning points. This structure provides clear goals and recognition for your contributions to the community.

At the Young Tree stage (400 points), you'll discover which type of tree you'll become, adding an element of excitement and personalization to your journey. As you continue to participate and contribute, your tree will blossom and reach mythical status!

Leadership Structure

Leaders' Responsibilities

Our regional leaders play a vital role in maintaining connections and organizing activities for the SEED Garden community.

  • Active Communication: Maintain ongoing contact with all Alumni from your representing country
  • Cultural Education: Prepare and deliver 2-3 lectures on Japanese cultural topics of your choice (e.g., food, history, arts)
  • Project Development: Contribute ideas and assistance to improve the overall project structure
  • Activity Moderation: Serve as moderators for both in-person and remote activities throughout the year
  • Network Building: Help connect alumni with similar interests across different cohorts
  • Feedback Collection: Gather input from participants to continuously improve the program

Japanese Alumni Integration

Japanese alumni serve as cultural bridges and vital resources for both current participants and international alumni.

  • Facilitating communication between teams and producers through translation assistance
  • Leading authentic cultural workshops that provide genuine Japanese perspectives
  • Maintaining connections with Yamanashi producers and providing local updates
  • Supporting current participants with practical knowledge of Japanese business culture
  • Assisting with logistics and cultural preparation for the Study Tour
  • Providing guidance on cultural etiquette and customs before the trip to Japan

Rewards System

The SEED Garden features a comprehensive rewards system that recognizes and celebrates your engagement and contributions. As you progress through growth stages, you'll unlock increasingly valuable rewards.

There are separate reward tracks for current participants (who will receive their rewards during the Study Tour in Japan) and alumni (who will receive rewards during Goro Mutsuura's visits to their countries or through international shipping).

The most exclusive rewards are reserved for those who reach the highest levels, creating healthy competition while ensuring everyone can achieve meaningful recognition for their participation.

Reward Distribution Policy

If more participants reach a growth stage than there are available rewards for that stage (e.g., 10 people reach Blooming stage but only 7 rewards are available), a random lottery system will be used in December to determine the winners.

For the exclusive Mythical Tree rewards, only one SEED 2025 participant and one Alumni member can win this prize. The first person from each category to reach this stage will receive the premium gift set.

How to Participate

Participating in the SEED Garden is easy and rewarding! Here's how to get started and make the most of this unique community:

  1. Register on the SEED Intranet: Ensure your profile is complete and up-to-date
  2. Attend the Welcome Ceremony: Join us on July 10th for the official launch
  3. Participate in Activities: Check the calendar regularly and join events that match your schedule
  4. Track Your Progress: Monitor your growth stage and points on the SEED Intranet
  5. Give Feedback: Help us improve by sharing your thoughts on activities and the overall program

Remember that your participation not only helps you earn rewards but also creates tremendous value for current SEED participants who benefit from your experience and insights. Together, we can create a vibrant, supportive community that extends far beyond the formal SEED Program timeline.

If you have any questions about the SEED Garden or how to get involved, please contact Alonso Hernandez, who serves as the Project Coordinator for this initiative.
